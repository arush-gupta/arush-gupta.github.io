Welcome to **Teaching Translated**, a podcast series that delves into the teaching methodologies of professors from leading research universities, with an initial focus on **Computer Science Education**. Through candid conversations with esteemed educators, we explore the art and science of teaching, and how innovative teaching practices shape student learning and foster critical thinking.

## Podcast Vision

In each episode, we uncover how educators inspire curiosity, encourage critical inquiry, and address diverse challenges in the classroom. Our guests share their personal teaching philosophies, how they foster inclusivity, and how they use failure as a tool for growth. We also discuss how empathy, humility, and curiosity over perfection are vital in creating environments where students thrive.

The podcast aims to provide insights into:
- Personal experiences and philosophies of professors in computer science.
- The emotional impact of education and how it affects learning.
- Approaches to fostering critical thinking, curiosity, and inclusivity in the classroom.
- The integration of natural science frameworks into teaching computer science.

## Episode Format

Before recording, we’ll have a brief 15–20 minute conversation to establish rapport and outline the structure of the podcast based on the professor’s responses to key questions. This conversation will ensure a smooth and engaging flow. The podcast format encourages candid responses, humor, and open discussion.

## Why Listen?

Whether you're an aspiring educator, a student of computer science, or simply someone passionate about the future of education, *Teaching Translated* provides valuable insights into the world of teaching. Our goal is to learn from the best educators, understand their teaching philosophies, and inspire both current and future educators to refine their methods and approach.

Join us as we explore how the best professors in the world are shaping the future of learning.
