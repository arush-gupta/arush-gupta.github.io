During a two-year gap in my academic trajectory, I embraced the opportunity to explore and reflect deeply on my personal and professional goals. This period was more than a break; it became a transformative time that enriched my perspective and skill set, ultimately shaping my readiness to tackle future research with renewed determination.

## Time of Reflection and Growth

This phase of exploration involved extensive travel and introspection. Through encountering diverse cultures and environments, I refined my research interests and developed a more adaptable mindset. One particularly impactful experience was learning to embrace turbulence during flights. Initially a source of anxiety, turbulence became a metaphor for navigating uncertainty. I learned to find calm amidst unpredictability, which reminded me that challenges—whether in travel or research—often lead to personal growth and a deeper appreciation of the journey.

## Skills and Adaptability

During this period, I cultivated skills that are crucial for research, including problem-solving, independent learning, and resilience. This time allowed me to gain insights into my ability to adapt, embrace uncertainty, and find joy in the process of exploration itself. The independence I developed and the challenges I faced have strengthened my resolve to return to academia with a focus on contributing meaningfully to the field.
